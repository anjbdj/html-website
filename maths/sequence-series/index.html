Sequence & Series

  1. An arithmetic progression (A.P.) : a, a + d, a + 2 d,..... a + (n - 1) d is an A.P. Let a be the first term and d be the common difference of an A.P., then nth term = tn = a + (n – 1) d
  2. The sum of first n terms of A.P. are

    rth term of an A.P. when sum of first r terms is given is tr = Sr – Sr – 1.

  3. Properties of A.P.

    If a, b, c are in A.P. => 2 b = a + c & if a, b, c, d are in A.P. => a + d = b + c.

    Three numbers in A.P. can be taken as a - d, a, a + d; four numbers in A.P. can be taken as a - 3d, a - d, a + d, a + 3d; five numbers in A.P. are a - 2d, a - d, a, a + d, a + 2d & six terms in A.P. are a - 5d, a - 3d, a - d, a + d, a + 3d, a + 5d etc.

    Sum of the terms of an A.P. equidistant from the beginning & end = sum of first & last term.

  4. Arithmetic Mean (Mean or Average) (A.M.):

    If three terms are in A.P. then the middle term is called the A.M. between the other two, so if a, b, c are in A.P., b is A.M. of a & c.
