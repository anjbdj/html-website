Let's discuss how much time it takes to crack IIT JEE ?

  • There a number of factors which are crucial to achieve success in IIT JEE.

  • Every student has different cognitive capabilities, so if one strategy works for student that same strategy may not work for the second.

  • To be very true, there is no fixed number of hours/months to crack IIT JEE.

  • IIT JEE is not about quantity or number of hours you put into studies. It's about the concepts. If you catch the concepts less number of hours are OK.

  • IIT JEE is about two things. First is to deeply know the concept, Second is problem solving based on the concepts.

  • So, Try to fully understand the concepts, learn the formulaes by heart and practice questions based on the concepts.

  • Previous Year questions from IIT JEE are very helpful as it helps you understand what is the concept the examiner is trying to check.

  • There may be many questions based on one concept. So try to get the concept properly.

  • These are the key points to be successful in IIT JEE.

  • Now, let's return to the topic of how much time ?

    Simple answer is about 4-5 hours of study with 40% of the time to the concepts and 60% to the problem solving.

    This routine if followed for 2 years will increase your chances of selection.

  • Also, 1 tip we would like to share is to very careful of the data and statistics of how much weightage does the chapter you are cuurently studying has in IIT in the past 5 years. Give more time to the chapters/concepts which have high weightage and are frequently asked.

  • Remember to keep the IIT JEE syllabus with you, Be very smart - don't give too much time to 1 concept/chapter. Your objective is not to be a Phd level person in 1 topic. Your aim is to get only as much knowledge where you can crack the exam. That's it.

  • In IIT you will sometimes find some questions from small topics given high wieghtage, and the students focus on such topics, which may not be a good strategy.

  • Try with the approach of High weightage and High Frequency asked questions.
